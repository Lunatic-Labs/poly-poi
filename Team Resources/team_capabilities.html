Low-key Landmarks is a technically capable, communication-forward team with a strong creative anchor. Three of four members can code, present, and collaborate cross-functionally — a solid baseline for a project that requires both building and pitching. Helene carries the design load almost entirely on her own (UI/UX, visual design, prototyping, storyboarding), which is a significant asset but also a single point of failure for that work. Hunter and Willie cover the technical and presentation layer well, with Hunter providing architectural depth and Willie adding slide and UI design overlap.

The team's most consistent gap is Data Analysis — a universal weakness across all members — followed by Academic Research, which three of four find challenging. Management and Organization is notably underdeveloped as a shared strength; no two members overlap here, and formal project structure will likely need to be deliberately assigned rather than assumed. Darrell brings soft skills the rest of the team lacks: a psychology background grounding user research decisions, strong public speaking for user-facing content, and broad AI tool familiarity. The team will be well-served by leaning into its communication and design strengths for deliverables, while proactively delegating analytical and research tasks where they arise.
